Current Research: Electric Propulsion Data Aquisition

With the help from Professer Timothy Takahashi, I have been able to establish a research project with ASU's Fulton Undergraduate Research Initiative (FURI). As part of FURI, I am in the works of developing a flight controller that logs data regarding the flight of an aircraft. Using a combination of in-flight filtration and post-flight analysis, I am looking to develop an effecient method to tune an electric aircraft to maximize certain Aerodynamic features.


The PCB in the image above was the first iteration of a flight controller that could potentially be used to collect and log sensor data. The sensors that will be equipped on the flight controller are:


  1. Acceleromter
  2. Gyro
  3. Pitot Tube
  4. Thrust Sensor (Load Cell)
  5. Potentiometer (Angle of Attack)

The current setup collects data at a sampling rate of 100Hz (every 10ms) which gives users plenty of data to work with. All data collected is also logged onto a micro sd card which can be analysized post-flight.


The screenshots above are snippets of the two layers of code running. One runs on the arduino to collect and log sensor data to the sd card. The other runs post flight on the SD card and simulates a digital and analog filter. This enables noisy analog data collected to be usable.
